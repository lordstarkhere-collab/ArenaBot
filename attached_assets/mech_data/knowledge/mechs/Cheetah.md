# Cheetah

The Cheetah is a Support mech known for its mine deployment capabilities and area damage, specializing in battlefield control. It features the ability to drop proximity mines for controlling enemy movement and dealing intense area damage, While usually not a recommended option, It can serve as a fun option for those seeking something different.

## Ability: Mines

*Cheetah's ability allows it to deploy up to 3 mines on the battlefield by throwing them on the ground a short distance infront of it, where they remain until triggered by a target stepping on them, removed by a system crash from guardian or outlaw, or removed when a mine is deployed while the 3 mines cap is hit, replacing the oldest mine, this ability has high damage potential and reliably surpassess mods, making it a good mech for the Mech Ability Damage Implant, A noteworthy drawback is the short mine deployment range and the mine deployment delay and explosion delay, cheetah can't deploy mines too far from its own position, so it has to get to where it wants its mines to be, something its HP and Speed don't really help*

## Strategy

*Use cheetah To deny a passage by blocking it with mines and preparing to catch anything that triggers them off guard, using splash weapons to hit the target around the corner when they trigger your mines for a high burst of damage, potentially killing them before you have to face them eye to eye, which is something cheetah wants to avoid due to the lacking defences.*

## Guide/Tips

### Usage

*Use cheetah carefully, treading around walls and cover and sticking with allies to help keep you safe, place mines on chokepoints that your enemies will have/want to walk by, like on beacons or on tight passages leading to them, try to keep exposure to a minimum, using indirect fire weapons or splash damage weapons or other low exposure options to avoid eye to eye combat to suffer the least from cheetah's lack of durability, cheetah is not a pushing mech, but rather a push denial, use it to keep the enemies from pushing against your team, but be mindful even then cheetah can be a rather dead wright, keep an eye on your mine icons next to the ability as they light up when triggered, notifying you even if you can't directly see them.*

### Countering

*The slow mine deployment cooldown and explosion delay, paired with the lower enerfy and durability make cheetah vulnerable especially upclose, and the mine delay also means that dashing or highly mobile mechs, like surge, nomad, eclipse or vortex can easily dash or sprint past the mines without taking damage, leaving the cheetah defenseless and open for the kill, seeker is another option as it will just teleport to the cheetah, but if given enough time to prepare past being locked on, the cheetah might prepare a mine trap for the seeker so be aware, lastly, another option to counter cheetah is splash and homing weapons, as cheetahs like to hide, using the radius of splash weapons or the curving of homing weapons to hit it in cover can be effective especially if catching cheetah off guard, a great choice here is storm racks, lastly, cheetah has no defense against status effects, a good daze, EMP, blind, or even stasis will make it a very easy target.*

### Builds

*Pair cheetah with indirect fire, low exposure, and splash damage weapons, Ideally the likes of Storm Racks, Pod guns, Or Nade Luanchers, allowing you to have the advantage of attacking the target before it attacks you, In terms of implants, cheetah doesn't benefit as much from the cooldown implant, due to an already very short cooldown of 6seconds being more than you need considering the 3 mine cap, Instead an ability damage implant will make a better pair to increase the already high mine damage, The radius implant can be useful but its very situational and generally not recommended, lastly, in terms of shield mods, a Perimeter Multilayer shield will serve best for cheetah.*
